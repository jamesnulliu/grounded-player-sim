EDNET-KT1 REAL RESPONSE-TIME TEST (2026-07-13)

Question: does the chess when-not-what pattern transfer to a second real
education response-time dataset after the ASSISTments timing negative?

Protocol was frozen before student-log ingestion in
scripts/ednet_manifest.json and documents/ednet_protocol.md. The official
README describes KT1 elapsed_time as milliseconds spent on the current
question, but upstream issue #5 leaves bundle averaging/attachment unresolved.
The primary cohort therefore uses only singleton-question bundles, where one
reported duration cannot be duplicated across several questions.

Pre-registered criteria:
  timing transfer: FAIL (pooled CI crosses zero; seed 2 is wrong-sign)
  full when-not-what: FAIL

VERDICT: honest timing null, robust response positive. EdNet independently
confirms that the evolving latent helps future real-student response prediction,
but it does not reproduce chess's timing-over-choice asymmetry. Together with
the ASSISTments response-time negative, real non-game timing transfer has now
failed in two education datasets. The paper must scope when-not-what to real
chess plus synthetic KT and describe strategic clock management as a plausible
mechanism, not an established cross-domain law.
