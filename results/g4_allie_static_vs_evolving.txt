ALLIE + STATIC-INDIVIDUAL VS ALLIE + EVOLVING (2026-07-14)

Question: after controlling for both a released think-time model and a stable
per-player calibration, does within-player evolution still add held-out timing
value?

Protocol:
  cohorts: 2017-04, 2019-07, 2021-06 (100 players each)
  seeds: 0--4; 15 epochs; session split; latent_dim=16; hidden_dim=64
  shared external predictor: Allie medium checkpoint, cached per decision
  shared offset: log(Allie prediction), locked, plus one global intercept
  static arm: learned per-player embedding, constant over the trajectory
  evolving arm: recurrent NeuralInjector over the player's history features
  metric: held-out timing NLL; signed effect = evolving - static
  pooling: average each player's paired effect over seeds, then bootstrap
  players. The cross-cohort summary first averages the one repeated username
  across cohorts, then bootstraps 299 unique players.

cohort    Allie    +static   +evolving   evolving-static   95% CI
2017-04   2.5421    2.5361    2.5191       -0.0170          [-0.0254,-0.0082]
2019-07   2.3349    2.3186    2.3172       -0.0014          [-0.0138,+0.0112]
2021-06   2.3054    2.2920    2.2726       -0.0194          [-0.0300,-0.0088]

Unique-player aggregate: evolving-static = -0.0126,
95% CI [-0.0188,-0.0061], P(D-B<0)=1.000, n=299.

Seed audit:
  2017-04: all 5 seeds favor evolving; every per-seed CI excludes zero.
  2019-07: 3 negative / 2 small positive; every per-seed CI crosses zero.
  2021-06: all 5 seeds favor evolving; every per-seed CI excludes zero.

VERDICT: the evolving latent adds significant timing value beyond both Allie
and a stable per-individual calibration on 2 of 3 cohorts. The 2019-07 cohort
is an honest null, matching its other strong-control results. This isolates a
within-player dynamic contribution more directly than Allie-vs-Allie+z alone:
the result is usually significant and cohort-dependent, not universal.
