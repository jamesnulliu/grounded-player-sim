VARIANCE-CONTROLLED, PLAYER-BOOTSTRAPPED CONCENTRATION (paper-readiness
audit P0 item): does the timing D-B edge really CONCENTRATE under time
pressure, or is the existing decision-level `run_concentration` mean gap
merely amplified because high-time-pressure decisions are a noisier regime
(and because it pools within-player decisions rather than treating the
player as the independent unit, contra design.md sec 5's own bootstrap
rule)?

Method: gps.experiments.ec.run_concentration_stratified. Same time_pressure
terciles as the original check, but (1) aggregates held-out nll_D-nll_B to
ONE point per player per bucket before bootstrapping (bootstrap over
players, not decisions), and (2) additionally reports that per-player mean
normalized by the bucket's pooled decision-level stdev -- a standardized
effect size a pure variance confound would shrink toward flat across
buckets, but genuine state-dependence would not.

Data: cached ingested cohorts g4_data/ec{2017,2019}/dataset.jsonl.gz (100
players, 2013-01-style Lichess blitz pipeline, 2017-04 and 2019-07). Same
cohorts as results/timing_concentration.txt. 2026-07-13.

  cohort    seed  raw ratio (hi/lo)  normalized ratio (hi/lo)  verdict
  2017-04   0     3.97x              2.72x                     survives
  2017-04   1     4.06x              3.55x                     survives
  2019-07   0     6.09x              2.78x                     survives
  2019-07   1     4.68x              3.07x                     survives

Every high-time_pressure bucket's 95% CI (bootstrap over players) excludes
zero, P=1.00, in all 4 runs. Low-time_pressure buckets are also significant
in all 4 runs (P=0.98-1.00); mid-time_pressure is marginal in 2/4 (P=0.82,
0.87) -- consistent with the original (non-monotone-in-the-middle) pattern
already reported.

VERDICT: the concentration effect SURVIVES variance control. Normalizing by
each bucket's own decision-level noise shrinks the raw ~4-6x gap to a
smaller but still clearly >1x standardized ratio (2.7-3.6x) in every run.
So the "the latent's timing edge concentrates under time pressure" reading
is not an artifact of high-time-pressure decisions simply being noisier,
and bootstrapping over players (the correct independent unit) rather than
decisions does not wash the effect out either -- it firms up the
state-dependence interpretation in paper_draft.md's Limitations section
rather than requiring it be softened to individualization-only.

Reproduce:
  from gps.data.store import load_dataset
  from gps.experiments.ec import run_concentration_stratified
  ds = load_dataset("g4_data/ec2017/dataset.jsonl.gz")
  run_concentration_stratified(ds, channel="timing",
      bucket_feature="time_pressure", n_buckets=3, seed=0).summary()
