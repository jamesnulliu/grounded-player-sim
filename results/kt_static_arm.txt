KT FITTED-STATIC-PROFILE ARM: DOES A FROZEN PROFILE SUFFICE? (2026-07-22)

Question: the KT shuffle control identified the response-channel gain as
order-invariant individualization. The sharpest objection that invites:
"then a STATIC profile also works -- the updating state is unnecessary on
KT." This arm measures that claim directly.

Arm S = logistic regression over [1, item_difficulty, student_profile],
where student_profile is ONE FROZEN NUMBER per student: their accuracy over
their own training split (>=35 observed answers; leakage-safe). This is the
strongest static profile constructible -- a fitted summary of real observed
behavior, far better than any hand-written persona sentence -- and it never
updates during evaluation. Same IRLS fit, same split, same frozen cohorts
(fingerprint-verified) as the memory arm; paired per student against the
cached B (memoryless twin), M (updating memory), D (evolving latent).

Tallies: S-B significantly POSITIVE (frozen profile WORSE than the twin)
6/8, never significantly better. M-S: memory beats the frozen profile
8/8. D-S: evolving latent beats it 8/8. The M-S margins (-0.011..-0.098)
are 1.5-2x LARGER than the headline D-B gaps themselves.

VERDICT: a frozen per-student profile is NOT enough on KT -- it is beaten
by the updating memory on every dataset and usually loses even to the
memoryless twin. The clean same-model-class pair is M vs S (identical
logistic fit; only frozen-profile vs updating-stats differ): 8/8,
decisively. Why: students DRIFT (they learn; Spanish vocabulary, the
strongest-drift dataset, is the extreme case at -0.098) and the last 30% of
a sequence sits at a different level than the first 70%, so a confidently
frozen estimate mis-calibrates where an updating one keeps tracking.

INTERPRETATION FIX this result forces: "order-invariant individualization"
(the shuffle control) does NOT mean "freezable individualization." A
running mean is order-invariant yet still updates -- and updating is
load-bearing. The KT scoping sentence becomes: the gain is a
CONTINUOUSLY-UPDATED estimate of who the student is (order-invariant, NOT
freezable); what KT does not show is the fast emotion-like flavor of the
state, which remains a chess-timing result.

KT now has its own complete mini-ladder, all instrument-verified:
  frozen fitted profile (S)  <  memoryless twin (B)  <  updating memory (M)
  ~= evolving latent (D)
i.e. even on the channel where the state converges toward a stable fact,
FREEZING the person fails; the simulator must keep estimating.

Caveats: S-vs-B is not same-model-class (B is the trained GRU twin whose
inputs include recent-window features; S is a logistic with no recency) --
read S-B as "a frozen profile even loses to a crude recent-window model,"
and rely on M-vs-S for the clean frozen-vs-updating contrast. M updates
through the evaluation period (teacher-forced filtering), which is exactly
the deployment difference being measured: "write a profile once" vs "keep
watching."
